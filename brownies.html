Sourdough Discard Brownies

This is my favourite sourdough discard brownie recipe: it's simple, it's quick, and it tastes delicious. The brownies are perfectly fudgy with crispy, shiny tops, with the tiniest notes of tanginess from the sourdough discard.

Sourdough Discard Brownies

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 35 minutes
  • Yield: 16-20 brownies

Ingredients

  • 340g semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 40g cocoa powder
  • 2 tsp (10g) vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 200g granulated sugar
  • 110g dark brown sugar
  • 125g sourdough discard
  • 120g all-purpose flour
  • 5g salt

Instructions

1. Preheat the Oven
Preheat the oven to 176Â°C and line a 9" x 9" metal baking dish with parchment paper.

2. Make the Chocolate Mixture
Melt the butter in a small saucepan over low heat. Add the chocolate chips, vanilla extract and dutched cocoa powder. Stir the ingredients until the chocolate chips have melted and the mixture is smooth. Remove the saucepan from the burner and set it aside to cool down.

3. Make the Batter
In a large mixing bowl, beat the eggs, brown sugar and granulated sugar with an electric mixer for 7-10 minutes. Add the sourdough discard and chocolate mixture to the bowl beat on low until combined. Add the flour and salt and stir with a spatula until just combined (do not overmix!).

4. Pour and Bake
Pour the batter into the baking dish and smooth the top. Bake for 35-40 minutes.

5. Cut and Serve
Let the brownies cool completely before cutting into squares. Enjoy!

Tips & Tricks

  • If you prefer your brownies less sweet and more bitter, decrease the amount of sugar in the recipe by 50g and increase the amount of cocoa powder.
  • To check if your brownies are done baking in the oven, do the toothpick test: poke a toothpick into the center of the cake and pull it out. If the toothpick comes out cleanly, it's done! If not, it needs more time.
