Sourdough Discard Flatbread

This multi-use flatbread can be used as naan, pita bread, or soft taco tortillas. It tastes so hearty and fresh; you'll never be able to go back to store-bought flatbread!

Sourdough Discard Flatbread

  • Prep time: 3-4 hours
  • Cook time: 1 minute
  • Yield: 8-16 flatbreads

Ingredients

  • 300g all-purpose flour
  • 100g sourdough discard
  • 14g olive oil
  • 200g water
  • 8g salt

Instructions

1. Make the Dough
In a large bowl, combine water, sourdough discard, and olive oil with a spatula and stir until the sourdough discard has disolved. Add flour and salt, mix until there are no dry bits.

2. Bulk Rise
Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let rest on the counter for 2-3 hours, or until the dough has more or less doubled in size.

3. Shape
Transfer the dough onto a floured surface and divide and shape into 8-16 balls. Cover with a towel and let rest for 20 minutes or until puffy.

4. Preheat the Skillet
Preheat an oiled skillet at medium-high heat.

5. Roll the Dough Balls
Dust a rolling pin with flour and roll out each dough ball into a very thin circle-ish shape.

6. Cook
Once visible steam is coming off of the skillet, cook the flatbreads one at a time, roughly 30 seconds per side. Enjoy!

Tips & Tricks

  • If using the flatbread for tacos, make each dough ball smaller and roll them out extra thin.
